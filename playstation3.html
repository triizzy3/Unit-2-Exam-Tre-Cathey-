The PlayStation 3 (PS3) is a home video game console developed and marketed by Sony Interactive Entertainment. The successor to the PlayStation 2, it is part of the PlayStation brand of consoles. It was first released on November 11, 2006, in Japan,[9] November 17, 2006, in North America, and March 23, 2007, in Europe and Australia.[10][11][12] The PlayStation 3 competed primarily against Microsoft's Xbox 360 and Nintendo's Wii as part of the seventh generation of video game consoles.

The PlayStation 3 had a rough start coming out in the gaming world due to other competitors at the time that had a better start such as the Wii and Xbox 360, high price, and lack of quality games. This reception would get better over time. Praise came for this model mainly because of the Blu-ray capabilities. This PlayStation was the first to integrate social gaming services, including the PlayStation Network, as well as the first to be controllable from a handheld console, through its remote connectivity with PlayStation Portable and PlayStation Vita. This model was lighter and thinner than the previous PS2 and had a minor startup change. PlayStation 3 also started the integration of the backward compatibility to use disc from past generations and run them. Sony originally had the Sixaxis controller that came with the PlayStation 3 which could detect motion in three directional and three rotational axes; six in total, hence the name Sixaxis. This controller lacked vibration capabilities, so they later released the DualShock 3 controller that had everything the same as the Sixaxis but was heavier and had vibration. Sony also included the ability for the console software to be updated by accessing a website or going through the console itself and could also connect to other portable PlayStation handheld consoles.
